Breakdown of Costs by Sub-Play

The cost of producing a barrel of oil and gas obviosuly varies across the world, setting up winners and losers as the price of crude fluctuates. But the costs can also vary within the same shale play. Given the fluctuation of oil prices, investors always want to make sure that the average cost to get the oil out of the ground is does not result in losses. Therefore, comparing different categories of production costs across sub-plays will help inform the right investment decision and make sure the production cost per barrel is not prohibitively high. While this chart is not normalized by BOE, it gives a good idea which sub-plays are less costly. Looking at Edwards Condensate and Karnes Trough, which we have identified as the sub-plays of the optimum EUR and production activity, we see that they appear to have relatively same development costs. Karnes Trough has slightly higher "Other Cost", though. (*Dataframe sorted by Other Cost). We can also note that Southeast Gas has very low proppant and low water, indicating a smaller completion method.
